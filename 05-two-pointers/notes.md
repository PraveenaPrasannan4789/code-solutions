## What are Two Pointers?

Definition:
Two pointers is a technique where you use two indices (or pointers) to traverse a data structure to solve a problem efficiently.

Pointers can move from the same direction or from opposite ends.
Usually helps reduce nested loops → faster than brute-force.
## Common Uses
Opposite ends
Example: Check if an array is a palindrome, or find two numbers that sum to a target.
One pointer starts at the beginning, another at the end, and you move them toward each other.
Same direction
Example: Move all non-zero numbers to the front (like your previous example).
One pointer tracks current element, the other tracks the next position to place a number.
